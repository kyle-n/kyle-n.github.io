Name: Boeing C-17 Globemaster III

Specs: Costs $218 million per unit
- 174 feet long
- 170-foot wingspan
- Powered by four Pratt & Whitney F117-PW-100 turbofan engines
- Carries up to 170,900 lb
- Designed to drop up to 102 paratroopers with equipment
- Used in the Afghanistan and Iraq wars

Name: Sikorsky UH-60 Black Hawk

Specs: Average procurement cost per unit in 2012 was $21.3 million
- Built specifically to fit in a C-130 Hercules
- Can carry 11 troops with equipment or 9,000 lbs by sling
- Used in Grenada, Panama, Somalia, Iraq and Afghanistan
- Two highly modified Black Hawks were used in the raid that killed Osama bin Laden
- The president rides in a VIP version of a Black Hawk called "Marine One" when he is on it

Name: C-130J Super Hercules

Specs: Costs $67.3 million per unit for the Air Force in 2014
- 97'9" long
- 132'7" wingspan
- Carries up to 64 troops or 42,000 lbs
- Used extensively in Iraq and Afghanistan
- Maximum speed of 417 mph
- Often sold to allied militaries

Name: Douglas C-47 Skytrain

Specs: Flown between Dec. 23, 1941, and the present day
- 63'9" long
- 95'6" wingspan
- Carries up to 28 troops
- Used extensively in the Pacific theater of World War II
- A special version built for paratroopers was used in for European assaults as well
- Nicknamed "gooney bird" by soldiers in the European theater"
